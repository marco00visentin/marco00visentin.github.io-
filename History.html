Android History

Foundation

2003

Google

2005

With the acquisition by Google for an amount close to 50 million dollars, Android begins to become an operating system for mobile devices. In 2010 the Google vice-president David Lawee defined the acquisition of Android the best deal that the company ever did.

The great competitor

2007

In January 2007 Apple launched the first iPhone disrupting the market with its high-performance touch screen. The launch of iOS forced Android to modify the operating system that they were developing.

Open Handset Alliance

2007

In November 2007 the Open Handet Alliance was unveiled. This is a consortium composed of several technology companies and led by Google, with the goal of developing "the first truly open and comprehensive platform for mobile devices". This consortium was formed by Google, some chipset makers (like Intel and Qualcom) and the brand that will release devices with the Android OS, such as Samsung, LG and Motorola.

First Smartphone

2008

In September 2008 the first smartphone running the new operating system hit the market: the HTC T-Mobile G1(also known as HTC Dream). This device had a physical keyboard, but its commercialization was still successful.

Google Play Store

2008

In October 2008, Google launched Android Market, containing an interesting number of applications and games for users. This was the ancestor of the worldwide known Google Play Store.

Samsung first device

2008

In April 2009 Samsung released its first smartphone with the Android OS, the Samsung Galaxy i7500. This release was a failure, but this device is the ancestor of the famous Samsung Galaxy S series. Nowadays Samsung is the company producing Android Smartphones with the biggest share in the global smartphone market.

Google Nexus

2010

In January 2010 Google launched the Nexus Series, a discontinued line of devices which run the original Android operating system without the different User Interface developed by the companies which produced the Android smartphones. This series had different manufacturers, namely HTC, Samsung, LG, Motorola, Huawei and Asus.

AndroidWear

2014

In March 2014, Google announced that it was entering the era of smartwatches, launching a version of its operating system exclusively for this type of device called AndroidWear, which will be renamed Wear OS in 2018. This OS was used in many devices of different producers and was compatible with both Android KitKat 4.4 and iOS 8.2 or higher.

Google Pixel

2016

In October 2016 Google launched its own series of smartphones, the Google Pixel Serie. This Serie replaced the Google Nexus one and is no more in collaboration with other brands.

Huawei HarmonyOS

2019

In May 2019 the Android operating system became entangled in the trade war between China and the United States started by the US president Donald Trump. As a result, Huawei was no longer able to offer the Google services on its phones and lost its market share world widely. In the summer of 2019, Huawei announced it would create an alternative operating system to Android known as Harmony OS. This is the first case in which a company which was producing Android phones developed its own operating system.

Google Rebranding

2019

In September 2019 Google release its version Google 10, with a huge rebranding for the company. From this version the Operating System versions are no longer called after sweets or dessert, but it is simply followed by a number. This Android version also brought a new brand identity, with a new logo, color palette and text font.
